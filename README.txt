			*15 Puzzle Game

Objective:
	Arrange the numbered tiles in order from 1 to 15 by sliding them into the empty space.

Rules:
	The game consists of a chosen grid size and one empty space.
	You can move a tile adjacent to the empty space into the empty space.
	Continue sliding tiles until all tiles are arranged in numerical order from 1 to 15 (in 4x4) with the empty space in the bottom-right corner.

			*Pong Game

Objective:
	Score points by hitting the ball past your opponent's paddle.

Rules:
	Two players control paddles on opposite sides of the screen.
	Use your paddle to hit the ball back to your opponent.
	If the ball passes your opponent's paddle, you score a point.
	The game continues until one player reaches the winning score.

			*Tic-Tac-Toe (X/O)

Objective:
	Get three of your symbols (X or O) in a row, column, or diagonal to win.

Rules:
	The game is played on a 3x3 grid.
	Players take turns placing their symbol (X or O) in an empty cell.
	The first player to get three of their symbols in a row, column, or diagonal wins.
	If all cells are filled and no player has three in a row, the game is a draw.

			*Jigsaw Game

Objective:
	Reassemble the scrambled pieces to form a complete image.

Rules:
	The game consists of an image broken into pieces.
	Drag and drop pieces to their correct location to complete the image.
	Continue until the image is fully reassembled.

			*Match Master

Objective:
	Match all pairs of cards.

Rules:
	The game consists of a grid of facedown cards.
	Flip two cards at a time to reveal their images.
	If the images match, the cards remain face up.
	If the images do not match, the cards are flipped back facedown.
	Continue until all pairs are matched.

			*Sudoku

Objective:
	Fill the grid with numbers so that each row, column, and 3x3 subgrid contains all digits from 1 to 9.

Rules:
	The game consists of a 9x9 grid divided into 3x3 subgrids.
	Some cells are pre-filled with numbers.
	Fill the empty cells with numbers from 1 to 9.
	No number can be repeated in any row, column, or 3x3 subgrid.

			*2048 Game

Objective:
	Combine tiles with the same number to reach the 2048 tile.

Rules:
	The game is played on a chosen grid size.
	Swipe to move all tiles in the chosen direction.
	When two tiles with the same number collide, they merge into one tile with the sum of their numbers.
	The game continues until you reach the 2048 tile or no more moves are possible.

			*Breakout

Objective:
	Destroy all bricks by bouncing a ball off a paddle.

Rules:
	Control a paddle at the bottom of the screen.
	Use the paddle to bounce the ball up toward the bricks.
	The ball destroys bricks it touches.
	Clear all bricks to win the level.
	The game ends if the ball falls below the paddle.
	If you finish all bricks you go to the next level (more bricks).

			*Space Adventure

Objective:
	Navigate your spaceship to avoid asteroids, collect stars for points, and gather power-ups while aiming for the highest score possible.

Rules:

	1-Spaceship Movement:
Use arrow keys to move the spaceship: left, right, up, and down.
You can also move the spaceship by dragging it with the mouse or touch input.

	2-Asteroids:
Asteroids will appear at the top of the screen and move downwards.
Avoid collisions with asteroids to prevent a game over.
If the spaceship collides with an asteroid, the game ends.

	3-Stars:
Stars will also appear at the top of the screen and move downwards.
Collect stars by colliding with them to earn points.
Each star collected increases your score by 10 points. If invincible, the score is doubled to 20 points per star.

	4-Power-Ups:
Power-ups appear randomly and move downwards.
Collect power-ups to become invincible for 5 seconds. While invincible, you can collect stars for double points without being affected by asteroids.

	5-Magnetic Power-Up:
Magnetic power-ups appear randomly and move downwards.
Collect magnetic power-ups to activate magnetic mode for 10 seconds, during which stars are attracted to your spaceship, and your spaceship automatically shoots bullets.

	6-Bullets:
In magnetic mode, your spaceship will shoot bullets upwards.
Bullets can destroy asteroids on contact, each destroyed asteroid increases your score by 5 points.

	7-Scoring:
Score points by collecting stars and destroying asteroids with bullets.
The score increases as you survive longer and collect more items.

	8-Game Over:
The game ends when your spaceship collides with an asteroid while not invincible.
A game over popup will display your final score and options to reset the game or quit to the main menu.

			*Color Memory Game Rules

Objective:
	The goal of the game is to remember and replicate a sequence of colors shown by the game. Each correct sequence increases your score and the difficulty of the next level.

Game Setup:
	The game starts with a grid of buttons, each representing a different color.
	Possible colors include red, green, blue, yellow, purple, orange, cyan, magenta, and lime.

How to Play:

Starting the Game:
	Begin by pressing the "Start" button. The game will display a message to get ready before starting the sequence.

Sequence Display:
	The game will show a sequence of colors, starting with one color. The color buttons will light up one at a time, showing the player the sequence to remember.
	Watch carefully as the colors light up in a specific order. The sequence will be displayed with a brief pause between each color.
	After the sequence is shown, itâ€™s your turn to replicate it.

Replicating the Sequence:
	Click the color buttons in the same order that they were shown.
	If you click the buttons in the correct order, the game will move to the next level with a longer sequence.
	Each correct sequence completion adds 10 points to your score.
	If you press the buttons in the wrong order, the game ends.

Levels:
	With each new level, an additional color is added to the sequence, increasing the difficulty.
	The game continues until you make a mistake in replicating the sequence.

Game Over:
	If you make a mistake while replicating the sequence, a "Game Over" message is displayed.
	Your final score will be shown, and you will have the option to play again or return to the main menu.

Scoring:
	Each correct sequence completion awards 10 points.
	The score is updated and displayed at the end of each level.

Tips for Success:
	Pay close attention to the sequence as it is displayed.

	Practice makes perfect, the more you play, the better your memory and recognition will become.

			Color Match Game Rules

Objective:
	The goal of the game is to match the displayed color name with the correct color button within a limited time. Each correct match increases your score.

Game Setup:
	The game starts with a grid of buttons, each displaying a different color.
	Possible colors include Red, Green, Blue, Yellow, Purple, and Orange.

How to Play:

Starting the Game:
	Press the "Start" button to begin the game. The game will display a color name at the top of the screen.
	You have 30 seconds to score as many points as possible by matching the color name with the correct color button.

Color Matching:
	The game will display a color name at the top of the screen.
	Click the button that matches the displayed color name.
	If you select the correct color, your score increases by 10 points, and a new color name will be displayed.
	If you select the wrong color, the game continues, but no points are added.

Time Limit:
	You have 30 seconds to play. The timer starts as soon as the game begins.
	The game ends when the timer reaches zero.

Game Over:
	When the time is up, a "Game Over" message is displayed with your final score.
	You can choose to restart the game or return to the main menu.

Scoring:
	Each correct color match awards 10 points.
	Your score is updated and displayed continuously during the game.
